# RCPE Research Methodology

The Research Cross-Pollination Engine (RCPE) is built on the principle of **analogical reasoning in large-scale scientific literature**. This document outlines our approach to identifying and validating cross-disciplinary connections.

## 1. Defining Cross-Pollination

Cross-pollination occurs when a solution from one domain ($D_s$) is applied to a problem in another domain ($D_t$). RCPE identifies these opportunities through two primary pathways:

### A. Methodology Transfer
Identifying a tool, algorithm, or experimental protocol in $D_s$ that addresses a structural bottleneck in $D_t$. 
*Example*: Applying deep learning architectures from image recognition to genomic sequence alignment.

### B. Conceptual Analogy
Identifying high-level systemic similarities between two different phenomena.
*Example*: Applying models of animal foraging behavior in ecology to optimize search algorithms in computer science.

## 2. The Retrieval Pipeline

To ensure scientific rigor, RCPE uses a "Faithful Retrieval" approach:
1. **Source Discovery**: Querying $D_s$ using semantic embeddings generated by models trained on scientific text (e.g., SPECTER).
2. **Contextual Grounding**: Retrieving the full methodology section (if available) to ensure the technical prerequisites are understood.
3. **Citation Validation**: Checking the "influence" of a paper within its own field before suggesting it as a cross-domain solution.

## 3. Novelty & Feasibility Scoring

Each generated hypothesis is scored on a scale of 0.0 to 1.0 across three dimensions:

- **Novelty ($N$)**: Measured by the inverse of the semantic similarity between the proposed hypothesis and the existing literature in the primary field.
- **Feasibility ($F$)**: Assessed by the availability of resources (datasets, code) and the technical barrier of adaptation.
- **Impact ($I$)**: Estimated based on the citation weight and historical breakthrough potential of the source methodologies.

## 4. Addressing LLM Hallucination

To maintain high fidelity, RCPE implements:
- **Mandatory Citations**: Every claim in a hypothesis must be linked to a real DOI or arXiv ID.
- **Evidence Verification**: A secondary "Critic" agent verifies that the retrieved papers actually support the proposed connection.
- **Fallback to Originality**: If no strong connection is found, the system reports a "High Uncertainty" status rather than generating a speculative link.
